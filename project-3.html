Description

PCM was already a known and trusted brand in the offline market. But when they wanted to expand into the online space, they needed a content strategy that could match their legacy.

I studied their brand tonality, audience behaviour and category psychology — and created a powerful mix of content formats like UGC, vox-pop, social-first campaigns and snackable reels.

We didn’t just post content.
We built digital presence, recall and relevance.

This push helped PCM move from offline dominance… to visible online demand — and eventually boosted sales on Instamart as well.

Campaign 1:

Mqsale on Instamart

Objective :

The main goal was to get sales on instamart, across india

Idea & Execution:

PCM Masale’s Instagram was heavy on commercial content and lacked real user voice. So we shifted the narrative — we started creating in-house UGC style videos with simple, relatable stories that directly connected to Instamart purchase behaviour.

Parallel to that, we designed regional-language graphic posts — each highlighting the masala that was most loved in that specific city. This instantly created a local connect, built trust, and made the brand feel like “our” brand — not just another national label.

This combination of UGC + regional storytelling played a huge role in driving Instamart sales and stronger digital recall.

Campaign 2:

Masala in Minutes

Objective :

The goal was to promote that masale is live on blinkit

Idea & Execution:

After successfully going live on Instamart, two months later PCM decided to launch on Blinkit as well — and they wanted to build hype around it. So instead of simply announcing “we are now on Blinkit”, I created a creative campaign idea called Masala in Minutes.

The concept was simple but fun: Order PCM Masale simultaneously from Instamart and Blinkit — and see which platform delivers first. If anyone received both deliveries at the exact same time, they would win a special PCM hamper.

There was only one condition: the entire ordering + receiving moment had to be filmed without any cuts. This campaign idea created genuine excitement, participation and virality — while subtly making the Blinkit launch feel fast, competitive, and worth trying.
